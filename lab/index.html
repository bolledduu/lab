Biomedical Engineering & Intelligent Systems

Next-Gen Computational Biomedicine

We are a new research group at Indiana University pioneering the intersection of High-Performance Computing, AI, and Cardiovascular Medicine.

Our lab focuses on developing massive-scale "Digital Twins" to revolutionize diagnostics and surgical planning through advanced simulation and data science.

Explore Our Workflows

Core Focus

Scientific Discovery at Scale

We leverage the power of supercomputing to solve complex physiological problems, moving from basic science to clinical application.

HPC & Hemodynamics

We develop massively parallel fluid dynamics solvers (HARVEY) to model blood flow in patient-specific geometries at micron-scale resolution. This allows us to simulate complex flow patterns that are impossible to measure in vivo.

AI & Machine Learning

Our lab utilizes Linear Mixed-Effects models and Deep Learning to predict fractional flow reserve (FFR) and assess side-branch hemodynamics instantly, bridging the gap between slow physics-based simulations and real-time clinical needs.

Immersive Medicine

We are creating semi-immersive and fully immersive Virtual Reality (VR) environments. These tools allow clinicians to 'step inside' the patient's anatomy, improving spatial understanding for complex surgical planning.

Technical Architecture

The "Digital Twin" Workflow

Our lab operates a proprietary pipeline that transforms standard medical imaging into actionable clinical insights. This process relies on a seamless integration of clinical data, high-performance computing, and advanced visualization techniques.

Workflow Steps

1. Input Data Acquisition

We begin by importing high-resolution patient imaging data, typically from Angiography or CT scans. This raw data forms the foundation of the patient-specific model.

2. 3D Reconstruction

Using automated algorithms, we generate a 3D mesh of the arterial tree. This step converts 2D imaging slices into a 3D geometry suitable for physics simulation.

3. Massively Parallel Simulation

The 3D geometry is processed by our HARVEY solver on supercomputing clusters. We simulate blood flow dynamics at an extremely high resolution to capture wall shear stress and pressure gradients.

4. AI Analysis & Visualization

Machine learning models analyze the simulation output to extract key hemodynamic features. Finally, the data is rendered in a VR environment or a web dashboard for clinical review.

System Infrastructure

Our framework is designed for fault tolerance and scalability, capable of handling terabytes of simulation data per patient cohort. The infrastructure consists of three main layers:

  • Data Layer This layer handles the secure storage of Protected Health Information (PHI) and DICOM imaging datasets. It ensures compliance with medical data standards and provides a robust backend for data retrieval.
  • Compute Layer The core processing engine interfaces with MPI-based solvers running on leadership-class systems (such as Summit and Frontier). This is where the heavy lifting of computational fluid dynamics occurs.
  • Application Layer The user-facing component includes Unity3D-based VR renderers and Python-based ML inference engines. This layer translates complex numerical data into intuitive visual formats for researchers and clinicians.
