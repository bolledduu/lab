About

I am an Assistant Professor of Informatics at the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering at Indiana University Bloomington. My research focuses on advancing patient care and clinical medicine through the development of novel digital health solutions that leverage artificial intelligence and high-performance computing.

Prior to joining IU, I was an Assistant Computational Scientist at Argonne National Laboratory, where I held the Margaret Butler Fellowship in Computational Science. I also worked with Google Research in the AI and Health Group, developing empathetic large language models for healthcare applications.

I received my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering from Duke University, where I developed massively parallel computational fluid dynamic algorithms for cardiovascular disease diagnosis under the supervision of Prof. Amanda Randles.
